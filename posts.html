CHRO / HR RISK 7/10 
Three things to test before you trust a first-time manager with a hard conversation.

Most leadership programmes test the first one. Almost none test the other two.

1) Knowledge.
Can they explain what the policy says? What fair process means? When HR needs to be involved?

E-learning will get them here. Multiple choice will get them here. Most programmes stop here.

2) Response under interruption.
Can they hold the structure of the conversation when the other person:
- Cries.
- Gets defensive.
- Says "I want this in writing."
- Pivots to "this is really about something else."

Workshops rarely simulate this honestly. Peers are too kind. Coaches are too rare.

3) Recovery after a misstep.
The manager will say the wrong thing in the first three minutes. The question is whether they notice, and whether they can bring the conversation back.

Anatomy of a harassment disclosure that goes badly in the first three minutes.

I have sat with three HR Directors this year who walked me through their worst-handled cases. The patterns repeat.

Minute one: The receiver tries to soften the room.
- "Are you sure you want to do this formally?"
- "I'm sure there's been a misunderstanding."

The discloser hears: she is not being believed.

Minute two: The receiver pivots to process.
- "Let me explain what happens next."
- "We will need to involve HR."

The discloser hears: this is now a procedure, not a moment.

Minute three: The receiver tries to control the narrative.
- "Please don't talk to anyone else about this while we investigate."
- "Try not to make eye contact with him in the meantime."

The discloser hears: I am the problem.

Each of these moves comes from good intentions.

In a tribunal transcript, they sound like victim-blaming, evidence-suppression, and gaslighting.

The standard fix is more training. Another module. A revised script.

The actual fix is rehearsal. Run your managers through three realistic disclosures a quarter, with feedback, and watch how fast minute one changes.

That is the only thing I have seen actually move the needle.
